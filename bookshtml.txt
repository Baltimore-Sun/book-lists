They didn’t look up.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>A man on a nervous filly rode out of the gate, having a hard time keeping her on task. She skittered on the cobbles, drifting and turning. She didn’t like the sound of the whip or the smell of fear in the air.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>A lantern appeared on the deck of a ship, and the twenty captives trudged across the road toward its inconstant light.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>The horseman and his partner stayed on the shore.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>The black men stopped on the gangplank, their chains thudded together like a terrifying drum.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>“C’mon! This way!” called the man on the boat. “C’mon, move!”</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>But the black men didn’t move.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>“Move, damn you!” cried the man standing on the shore.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>The filly skittered sideways.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>One of the chained called out: “Has the bell rung?”</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>In unison, the reply came: “The bell done rung!”</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>“I said, ‘has the bell rung?’” His voice was strong.</p><p style='margin-bottom:16px;'>The filly was spinning around. With each bound forward she gained momentum, until she crashed straight into a wrought iron fence. He stood at the edge of the cliff, then squatted to lessen the blows of the buffeting wind.
